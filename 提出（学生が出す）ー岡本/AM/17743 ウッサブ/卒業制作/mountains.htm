MOUNTAINS

Nepal is known as the land of Himalayas world over. There are 1,310 peaks above 6000 meters of which 238 are already opened for climbing. Likewise, out of fourteen 8000 meters above peaks eight of them, including the highest peak in the world, Mount Everest lie in Nepal.

Mount Everest

Everest is a part of the Mahalangur Himalayan range and it lies in the Khumbu region of Nepal. It is named after Sir Gorge Everest and consists of two main climbing routes, one from Nepal and another from Tibet.

On a clear days from October till December abd March till April, following Himalayas are visible from Pokhara city Himal Chuli 7893m, Manaslu 8156m, Pabil GIV 7102m, Ganesh II 7150m, Ganesh V 6950 m, Lang tang 7246m, Gang Chhenpo 6297m, Sisa Pangma 8013m.

However, we recommend clients to go for Nagarkot (35 kms) from Kathmandu where you will get a chance to even view Mount Everest in the very east if the weather is very clear. The mountain views are weather permitting, hence the view is directly impacted by the weather. The best time to see the views of the Himalayas are in the mornings. It is the time when the air will not have warmed up so you will have a clear views, as the sun rays touched the ground it creates the heat and lifts the air obstructing the views of the mountains.
